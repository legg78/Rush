Debt repayment priority

A debt repayment priority is configured via Repayment priority service term. In the term settings weight of each debt type is set by the modifier.

Below is a debt repayment priority:

1.Bank costs — for example, charges due, fines and penalties are accumulated over compulsory payments and paid off in the first place, regardless of the occurrence date.

2.Overdue debt — for example, insurance fee, technical overdraft, fees, technical overdraft interests, principal debt interests, principal on purchasing transactions, etc.

3.Prompt debt — for example, insurance fee, technical overdraft, fees, technical overdraft interests, principal debt interests, principal on purchasing transactions, etc.

4.Current debt — for example, insurance fee, technical overdraft, fees, technical overdraft interests, principal debt interests, principal on purchasing transactions, etc.

In the case of overpayment (depositing funds in the amount greater than the total debt), the balance can be transferred to the customer’s own funds (bank account) immediately and will be available for use. It may also be retained in a special sub-account for a separate accounting of payments, in this case the amount will not be available for  customer use. This option can be set through the Use own funds service term.

Various conditions can be imposed on the usage of own funds for the repayment. This is set by the Repayment condition service term.

If you change the repayment priority, the adjustment is applied only to the new transactions made during the loan.
