Account structure

The system uses an account type directory structure: accounts of a specific type can be opened for specific entities. An account is identified by the account number, and the value of the account number is assigned to the account ID attribute.

Various types of balances can be defined for an account type. The balance structure derives its attributes from the accounting scheme and may be modified during system deployment.

The information that is available in an accounts report, can be detailed per sub-account. This is important for accounting transactions because they contain information that determines which balances should credited or debited. Institution and agent accounts are generated automatically according to the defined account structure.

Assume, for example, that a credit account of the credit_account type exists in the system. Two balances will be registered in the system for accounts of this type:

•Active — ledger cash

•Credit — credit funds

The sum of these balances is the account available balance.

The following values are specified for each account:

•currency (generally, it is acceptable for the balance to have a currency other than the currency of its associated account)

•status

•impact on the available balance, for example positive, negative, or neutral.

•etc.

Note: There are no limits to the number of account balances that can be linked to an account.
