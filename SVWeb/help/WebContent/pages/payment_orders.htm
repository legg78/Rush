Payment orders and recurring payments

SmartVista supports various methods for transferring funds with external sources such as payment networks, as well as secondary channels (for example, aggregators) for payment orders made by customers.

Payment order services must be set up to define which types of payments, direction of funds flow, and third-party service providers will be used by the system. Fees and other payment parameters should also be set up for each of these providers.

For intra-bank and inter-bank transfers, virtual service providers must be configured.

During payment processing, payments are transmitted to the service provider through its payment gateway. A payment gateway is an external connection/host defined for sending and receiving funds transfer messages.

One or more payment gateways may be defined and routing and selection rules are assigned to each connection to determine which gateway a particular message will be sent through. Routing and selection criteria include:

•Minimum commission rate

•Maximum fee rate

•Gateway priority

Each algorithm is implemented as a separate procedure.

 

 

The algorithm used to determine the gateway, a specific set or payment parameters (details necessary for the correct processing of payment by the external system and the identification of the final recipient/the sender of cash) is set on the bunch type of service – provider of service (the purpose of payment).

The purpose of payment defines the following:

•The services type and the service provider allocation

•The gateway determination algorithm

•A specific set or payment parameters — details necessary for the correct processing of payment by the external system and the identification of the final recipient/the sender of cash

Additionally, for payment level you can specify whether parameters are mandatory, editable, or specified in the template.

The list of parameters used for payments, is maintained in the system as a single directory.

Periodic recurring payments may also be set up for customers with regular payment patterns. Templates that contain payment parameters (amount and currency are mandatory), types of services, client preferences, and other payment options need to be configured for such payment type.

These payments can be initiated automatically by the system according to a schedule which can be specified for each template. The customer can choose the frequency (monthly, weekly, or custom) for each payment specified.

To configure payment schedules, the following parameters are required:

•An event trigger — this may be an event generated by the system, or a cycle chosen by the customer

•Payment amount calculation method

•Number of retries, in case of insufficient funds, as well as the time delay between attempts

In case of error or default, a template is marked as inactive and will be skipped by the scheduler until a correction is made.

A payment requirement is created on the basis of the payment template and the rules defining the how a payment is generated when a payment event is occurs in the system. A payment requirement can be created manually by a bank customer or an operator.
