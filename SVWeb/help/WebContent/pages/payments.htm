Loan payments

When a customer’s account is credited with an amount from an active credit service, the transaction is recorded in the credit module as a payment. The credit can appear in the form of a normal customer credit, a refund, a transaction reversal, or a credit adjustment.

A loan debt repayment may occur immediately after its payment (registration), or after the billing period closes (and includes the repayable debt).  This behavior is defined by the accessing the Repayment condition product setting and adjusting the service term.

Debts that must be settled upon repayment have a set order called the Repayment priority, which is determined by the debt parameters (for example, operation type, terminal type, or balance type). The payment amount is distributed between the debt balances according to this order.

A part of debt that comprises a part of the mandatory payment is singled out for each debt type defined in the Credit life cycle. In this way, the mandatory debt is paid while fulfilling the repayment on the main debt. If two debts hold the same priority, the debt with the earliest creation date is prioritized over other debts.

Where a reversal occurs, the repayment priority is ignored and the original transaction is repaid first. Interest on the original transaction is recalculated on the date of the reversal, or completely canceled if the reversal consists of the entire transaction amount.

In the case of reimbursement, the system does not check the original transaction and interest calculation. Reimbursement is applied as a regular payment and applies to the debt repayment priority.

When a dispute is opened, the event corresponding to the dispute is registered in the system. This event instructs the credit module to suspend the debt. After the debt is suspended, interest amounts are not charged and the debt is not included in the statement. The debt can be paid automatically; in this case, the disputed amount will be available for the customer to use.

One of the following occurs after a dispute closure:

•The disputed debt closes with the Reversal status, due to disputed amounts reimbursed. For example, the disputed debt reverses on account of the bank’s losses

•The debt becomes Active again and the balances amount returns its state prior to the dispute

Following a debt dispute, the debt continues to exist until it is fully repaid; interest amounts charged on the debt are calculated from the time of the creation of the debt until the time of the credit service activation.
