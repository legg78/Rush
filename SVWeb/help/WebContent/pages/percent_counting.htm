Interest statement and accrual

The interest rate on a credit service is set by the Interest rate service term. Its size is defined by the fee calculation functionality (taken from the history of the debt) and by modifiers. The size of the fee for each balance is displayed separately.

Calculated interest amounts can be recorded on a separate balance, with allowances for calculating additional percentages on the separate balance as well as on any other balance. For example, on delay the interest accrues both on the principal debt and on interests on the credit.

Interest accrual is a posting on the corresponding balance accounts and it occurs discretely. There is an interest balance per a balance: a balance for interests on the corresponding balance.

Interest accrues in the following situations:

•Periodically, in accordance with a predefined period (see the Interest charge period service term). The period may coincide with the invoicing period or differ from it (for example, if the calculation performed on the last working day and the size of accruals takes the entire accounting period into consideration)

•On repayment

•On setting a forced interest period (see the Forced interest charge period service term), for example, at the end of each month

•On closing the credit service

The date of an interest statement depends on the settings of the grace period:

•If there is no grace period, the interest shall accrue on the date of the end of the billing period in which the operation fell.

•If there is a grace period, the interest on transaction made in the previous billing period will be accrued on the grace period expiry date. This rule applies only to transactions in the previous billing period.

•For transactions in a later grace period: interest is always accrued on the end of a billing period.

The interest for each debt calculated  separately during a transaction (for example, purchase using credit funds). The fee that corresponds to the debt will be calculated.

The interest calculation period is regulated by following interest charge settings:

•Interest calculation start date — determines the start date and its type. Possible date types are:

oPosting date — interest is calculated starting from the date when the transaction is posted to the account

oTransaction date — interest is calculated starting from the date when the transaction is performed

oInvoicing date — interest is calculated starting from the billing date

oCurrent settlement date — interest is calculated starting from the settlement date set in the system (see Configuring a settlement day)

•Interest start date transformation — interest is calculated based on the date only, and does not take the time into consideration. This parameter determines a time shift used to adjust the specified date accordingly:

oStart of day

oReal time

oEnd of day
