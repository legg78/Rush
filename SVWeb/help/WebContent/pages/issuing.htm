Issuing

The diagram below depicts the interaction among the principal entities involved in issuing activities:

 

Entity

Description

Issuer, institution

The bank that issues cards and manages customers’ cards and accounts.

Agent

A branch or other unit of the issuing bank.

Customer (account owner),
an individual person or legal entity

Personal and contact data relating to a customer. A customer can have any number of accounts in a bank branch.

Legal entities (corporate customers) are registered via the Company system entities. This entity has a hierarchical organization that is useful for reporting. A company can have its own set of addresses and contacts. A corporate customer provides cards to card holders to provide access to its accounts (corporate cards) and can replenish the accounts of individuals from its accounts.

Contract

The contract that is opened in the branch bank. Accounts are created in an institution specified in the contract, and may be served in any branch.

Account

Bank account opened at a branch of the issuing bank.

Card

Card linked to the account. Cards may be plastic (magnetic stripe or chip cards), or virtual.

Several cards can be linked to one account and one card can provide access to multiple accounts of the customer.

Cardholder,
individual person

A cardholder may or may not be an account holder at the bank. An account may have more than one card issued to different cardholders.

Personal and contact details are registered for each cardholder.

An example of card use: a company issues cards to its employees. Corporate cards are issued for company account/accounts. In the payroll card program, accounts are opened for each employee, and employees’ accounts are replenished from the account of the company.

Card instance,
plastic card / virtual card

The card instance provides the means of accessing a customer account at an ATM, POS terminal, or online.

One card object can have several instances simultaneously. Each card instance has a validity period and a sequence number (first issued instance of this card, second instance, and so on). After the expiration of an instance of a card, the next card instance is issued.

Virtual instances do not have a physical embodiment.

Accounts, contracts, and card numbers are unique within the bank.
