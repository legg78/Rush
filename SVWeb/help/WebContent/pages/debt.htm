Debt

Debt is characterized by a combination of balances on which its total amount is distributed. For example, overlimit debt, past due debt, interest, etc. The number of possible balances is set in accordance with the rules of the particular bank.

A debt state changes whenever the amount of one or more of its balances changes. Every change is logged in the history of states. This history is used for fee calculations.

The total amount of debt is distributed in stages (periods): from the current debt to the debt of the oldest period, equal to the age of debt. If the debt age is zero, then the total debt is a current debt.

The period of debt is proportional to the period of invoicing.

A customizable list of actions can be assigned to each debt period. For example: during the first period of debt - set penalties, for the second period of debt - set penalties, block the card and put the entire amount of the debt to the mandatory repayment, in the third period of debt - notify the customer that his debt was transferred to the collection agency. Penalties to unpaid debts are applied according to the age of debt and the penalty period. The list of penalty actions is preconfigured in the system and cannot be changed. 

A certain amount of debt becomes overdue if it was declared as mandatory to repayment and has not been paid on time. Such debt realized by the transfer of the unpaid amount of mandatory amount due to the appropriate balance of overdue funds: the overdue type balance and the need fund transfer is given in credit account scheme.

Debt is liquidated when the amount of funds for all its balances is zero. For more information about debt statuses, see Viewing debts.
