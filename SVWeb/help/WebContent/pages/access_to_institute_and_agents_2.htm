Configuring roles

Configuring roles involves the following steps:

1.Determining relevant job positions within the organization and then defining sets of privileges for each position.

2.Grouping these sets of privileges into roles.

3.Organizing the roles into a hierarchy based on the sets of privileges assigned within them and reflecting the organizational hierarchy.

4.Configuring the roles with the fewest privileges, that is, the roles at the bottom of the organizational hierarchy.

5.Configuring the roles with more privileges in the hierarchy. These roles may have the same privileges as the previous ones plus several additional privileges.

6.Repeating the previous step for the next role within the organization.

The result is a nested structure of roles reflecting the organizational structure.

If the job performed by an employee requires access to the tasks performed by other employees working in different, typically subordinate, positions, the recommendation is that these roles be included as subroles of the superordinate role. For example, a supervisor role might include the role created for the employees working with customer claims, as well as the role created for customer service representatives (CSR). Two subordinate roles are included within the superordinate one. If the job responsibilities of the superordinate role are not fully covered by the subroles, the missing privileges can be added directly to the superodinate role.

This approach helps to minimize errors that occur when the same privilege is added several times to different roles. It also helps to visualize the differences in privileges between the roles. A single privilege required by multiple roles only needs to be added to the most subordinate subrole within the hierarchy.
