Invoicing

The bank provides the customer with an invoice for payment at the end of each billing period. The Invoice reveals the interest that has accrued at the time of invoicing.

An Invoice includes all debts unpaid at the time of invoicing (billing date): debts incurred in the closed period (new) and the debt incurred in prior periods. The total debt of the account and mandatory payment is calculated as a sum of the corresponding amounts of debts included in the bill.

On invoice forming the following periods are taken into account:

oPeriod of invoice forming (billing period, the Interest charge period service term)

oPeriod of mandatory payments (the Due date period service term)

oGrace period (the Grace period length service term)

oThe period of sanctions in case of non-payment (the Penalty charge period service term)

IMPORTANT: All of these periods must be less than or equal to the billing period.

The main invoice characteristics are mandatory amount due (mandatory payment) and period for payment of the mandatory amount due. Additionally, a grace period may be given during which, in the case of full repayment of the claimed debt, the borrower is exempt from paying interests for the use of credit.

Accrued interests cannot be undone, so if there is a grace period it is recommended to set the accrual of interest for the entire period of the credit on the date following the grace period. For example, the client made a purchase by credit funds and paid the current debt within 40 days (grace period - 50 days, billing period - 30 days. This debt will not be considered in the invoice neither for the first billing period (since the grace period is not yet complete), or in the second (because at the time of invoicing debt has already been repaid).

Due date period can be equal or less then penalty period. In this case, the borrower has additional time to make the payment before sanctions (for example, charge a penalty, blocking card, blocking account, reset credit limit) and non-paid mandatory payment will be imposed on delay.

Payment Date is only used in the statement the customer as the date by which he is obliged to make a mandatory payment. The onset of this date does not trigger any events in the system.
